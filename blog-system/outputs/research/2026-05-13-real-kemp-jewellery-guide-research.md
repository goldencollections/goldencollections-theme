## Wiki Ground Truth

Golden Collections defines real kemp jewellery as a premium South Indian temple/dance jewellery range for Bharatanatyam, Kuchipudi, bridal, and traditional festive use. It typically uses antique gold-plated brass or copper bases, handcrafted temple motifs, and red, green, or white Kemp/Kempu stones.

Confirmed facts for public use:

- Real kemp is a separate premium range from regular Bharatanatyam and Kuchipudi jewellery.
- Real kemp full sets usually cost INR 30,000 to INR 2 lakh, depending on components, workmanship, stone setting, and finish.
- Real kemp uses brass with 24 carat high gold plating and copper.
- Real kemp Bharatanatyam pieces usually use 1 to 3 microns of high-quality plating.
- Customer-facing copy should say `Kemp stones` or `Kempu stones`.
- Regular Bharatanatyam and Kuchipudi full sets usually cost INR 2,700 to INR 10,000.
- Regular Bharatanatyam and Kuchipudi jewellery uses alloy metal.
- Golden Collections started in 2012 and is led by Anil Tunk in Secunderabad/Hyderabad.
- Regular, premium kemp, and deity jewellery are made through partner artisans.
- Safe trust claims include handcrafted, hand-finished, handmade, artisan-made, temple craft, and Hyderabad atelier.
- Quality checks include stone quality, gold plating, finishing, joints, packing, and related checks before shipping.
- Golden Collections does not provide certificates.

Historical and cultural claim handling:

- The pillar can say real kemp belongs to South Indian temple/dance jewellery styling because this is already in the KB.
