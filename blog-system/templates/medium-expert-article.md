# Template: Medium Expert Article

## Purpose

Create a unique first-person Medium article that supports Golden Collections' external EEAT/entity signals without duplicating the on-site canonical guide.

This template is for owner-experience publishing, not a Shopify buying guide.

## Mandatory Intake Gate

Do not draft a Medium expert article until the intake contains at least five owner-supplied raw observations from Anil Tunk or the owner.

The observations can be rough notes. They do not need to be polished.

Acceptable examples:

- what customers most often misunderstand about real kemp jewellery
- what the owner checks when a batch comes from partner artisans
- how parents describe their confusion before arangetram
- what separates a well-matched set from a mismatched set in practice
- what buyers outside India ask before ordering
- what quality issues the store checks before dispatch
- how real kemp buying has changed since Golden Collections started in 2012

If fewer than five owner observations are provided, stop and ask for more owner notes before drafting.

## Recommended Title Frame

`What Selling Real Kemp Jewellery Since 2012 Has Taught Me About Choosing the Right Set`

Use `since 2012`, not a calculated year count.

## Ideal Use Case

Use after the on-site pillar page is live or ready:

- `Real Kemp Jewellery: Meaning, Materials, Uses, and Buying Guide`

The Medium article should point readers toward the on-site pillar with one natural link. It should not be a copied or lightly rewritten version of the pillar.

## Voice

First person from Anil Tunk's perspective.

Voice qualities:

- practical
- specific
- calm
- experience-led
- buyer-helpful
- not salesy
- not credential-heavy

Avoid Add to Cart language, discount language, and collection-heavy shopping copy.

## Word Count Range

800-1,200 words.

## Required Content Elements

1. First-person opening based on direct experience.
2. 3-5 concrete lessons learned from real customer or product experience.
3. One natural Golden Collections brand reference.
4. One link to the on-site real kemp pillar page.
5. Clear explanation of one or two common buyer misunderstandings.
6. Practical closing advice for buyers.

## Link Rule

Include only one natural link to the on-site pillar page unless the owner explicitly asks for more.

Preferred link destination:

`https://www.goldencollections.com/blogs/jewellery-guides/real-kemp-jewellery-guide`

If the pillar is later promoted to a Shopify page, update the link to the final canonical URL.

## EEAT Rules

Show experience through specific observations, not generic claims.

Good:

- "Many parents ask whether every red-and-green dance set is real kemp. That question usually tells me they are comparing two different ranges without realizing it."
- "When we check a set before dispatch, the matching of finish and stone color matters as much as the beauty of one individual piece."

Weak:

- "We are experts in beautiful traditional jewellery."
- "Our jewellery is the best and most traditional."

## Fact Rules

- Use facts confirmed in `knowledge-base/wiki/business-entity.md` and `knowledge-base/wiki/real-kemp-jewellery.md`.
- Use `Kemp stones` or `Kempu stones`.
- Do not use certificate language.
- Do not claim certificates are provided.
- Do not publish regular-range plating thickness unless owner-confirmed.
- Do not make specific historical origin claims unless sourced and owner-approved.
- Do not calculate years dynamically; use `since 2012`.

## Structure

1. Opening: what the owner has learned from selling real kemp jewellery since 2012.
2. Lesson 1: buyers often confuse real kemp with regular kemp-style dance jewellery.
3. Lesson 2: the full set matters more than one beautiful piece.
4. Lesson 3: material/range clarity prevents wrong expectations.
5. Lesson 4: performance buyers should start with use case and component list.
6. Lesson 5: care and storage determine long-term usefulness.
7. Closing: practical advice and one link to the Golden Collections real kemp pillar.

## Stop Conditions

Stop before drafting if:

- fewer than five owner observations are available
- the requested title uses a calculated number of years instead of `since 2012`
- the user asks for unverified history or certificate claims
- the piece is becoming a duplicate of the on-site pillar instead of a first-person experience article
