## Confirmed Facts From Google

- Google announced Search agents at Google I/O 2026 on 2026-05-19.
- Search agents will let users create, customize, and manage multiple AI agents inside Search.
- The first type is `information agents`.
- Information agents operate in the background, monitor changing information, and send synthesized updates with links and the ability to take action.
- Google says information agents can look across the open web, including blogs, news sites, and social posts, plus fresher data such as finance, shopping, and sports.
- Google says information agents will launch first for Google AI Pro and Ultra subscribers in summer 2026.
- Google also announced agentic booking in Search and agentic shopping capabilities connected to the Shopping blog announcement.
- Universal Cart is planned as a proactive shopping cart across Google surfaces, starting with Search and the Gemini app in the U.S. in summer 2026, with YouTube and Gmail to follow.
- Google says Universal Cart will use UCP for checkout and can transfer shoppers back to the merchant site where needed.
- Google says UCP can support cart actions, real-time catalog details such as variants, inventory, and pricing, and Identity Linking for loyalty/member benefits where integrated.
- Google says it is simplifying UCP onboarding in Merchant Center over the coming months.
- Shopify says it co-developed UCP with Google as an open standard for AI agents to connect and transact with merchants.

## Google Search Guidance That Still Applies

- Google's Search Central AI optimization guide says there is no special schema.org markup required for generative AI Search.
- Structured data should continue to be used as part of normal SEO and rich-result eligibility, not treated as a special AI-only hack.
- The practical Google-aligned path remains: crawlable indexed pages, useful content, accurate product/business data, clear structure, good page experience, images/video where useful, and Search Console monitoring.

## Agent-Friendly Website Guidance

Google's web.dev guidance says agents may understand a site through screenshots, raw HTML, and the accessibility tree. For Golden Collections, this means theme and product-page work should preserve:

- semantic buttons and links
- stable layout for product actions such as add to cart, variant choice, search, filters, WhatsApp help, and checkout handoff
- visible, unblocked interactive elements
- useful labels on inputs and controls
- clean page hierarchy and accessibility tree
