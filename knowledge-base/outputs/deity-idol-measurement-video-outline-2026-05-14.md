# Video Script / Shot List: How to Measure Your Idol for Deity Jewellery

Draft status: Authority-support outline for Golden Collections. Use as a short guide video, reel, or product-page embed.

Source facts used: Golden Collections is a Secunderabad/Hyderabad specialist for deity jewellery and Hindu idol alankaram; deity fit depends on idol size, placement, posture, face/head width, crown style, and measured ornament dimensions; custom deity jewellery is accepted by phone order; quality checks include stone quality, gold plating, finishing, joints, and packing before shipping.

## Goal

Help temple committees, priests, and devotees send clear idol measurements before buying deity jewellery such as crowns/mukut/kireedam, short necklaces, long harams, earrings, vaddanam/waist belts, tilak/namam, hands, legs, arches, and other alankaram accessories.

## Format

- Length: 60-90 seconds.
- Tone: Calm, devotional, practical.
- Visual style: Real hands measuring an idol with a soft measuring tape; close-ups of each measurement point; no exaggerated claims.
- CTA: Share measurements and idol photos with Golden Collections for fit help or custom deity jewellery.

## Measurement Checklist To Show On Screen

- Idol height in inches.
- Head or face width for crown fit.
- Crown space: inner width, depth, height, and arc/circumference if known.
- Neck/chest width for short necklace and long haram.
- Waist width after dressing for vaddanam/oddiyanam.
- Placement photos for earrings, nose ring, tilak/namam, hands, feet, arch, and base.
- Any posture issue: seated idol, trunk direction, hairstyle, weapons, flute, peacock feather, or dress bulk.

## Accuracy Notes

- Do not call any ornament universal. Say "fit depends on size and placement."
- Use regional names naturally: crown/mukut/kireedam, vaddanam/oddiyanam, haram, namam/thiruman, tilak.
- Avoid certificate language. Use quality-check language only.
