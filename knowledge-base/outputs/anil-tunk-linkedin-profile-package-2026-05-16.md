### Post 3 - Deity Measurement Guidance

Before choosing deity jewellery, take clear front and side photos and note the important measurements:

- Idol height
- Face or head width
- Crown clearance
- Shoulder and chest width
- Hand or weapon position
- Altar or mandapam depth
- Backdrop or arch space

These simple checks help avoid many fit problems, especially for crowns, harams, kavacham-style ornamentation and festival alankaram.

https://www.goldencollections.com/pages/how-golden-collections-checks-deity-jewellery-fit

### Post 4 - Real Kemp Guidance

Real kemp jewellery is a premium choice for Bharatanatyam, Kuchipudi and temple jewellery use cases. In my hands-on experience, real kemp pieces of similar size often feel noticeably heavier and more substantial than regular Bharatanatyam/Kuchipudi jewellery because of the brass and copper base.

That weight can be part of the premium stage presence, but it should be considered carefully for young dancers or long performance days.

Our guide:
https://www.goldencollections.com/blogs/jewellery-guides/real-kemp-jewellery-guide

### Post 5 - Varalakshmi Season Pipeline

Varalakshmi Vratham and Varamahalakshmi season is one of the most important times for deity jewellery and Amman alankaram planning.

For Varalakshmi, customers usually need to think about face size, crown scale, hastham and padam placement, vaddanam/waist belt fit, thomala or shoulder garland spacing, altar depth and the final flower/decor setup.

We are also documenting real alankaram examples with permission so customers can learn from actual setups, not only product photos.

https://www.goldencollections.com/blogs/jewellery-guides/varalakshmi-alankaram-checklist-2026
