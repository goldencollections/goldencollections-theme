# Recognition Outreach Target Plan - Deity And Bharatanatyam Jewellery

Generated: 2026-05-20

## Executive Read

Golden Collections should not chase random directories or generic backlink lists. The strongest recognition path is:

1. Publish one genuinely useful outreach asset on Golden Collections first.
2. Pitch respected cultural/editorial outlets with story-led angles.
3. Build school, temple, and festival partnerships where the audience already needs deity jewellery, Bharatanatyam jewellery, Kuchipudi jewellery, real kemp, or festival alankaram.
4. Use forums and social communities only as helpful participation, not advertising drops.

The two strongest commercial lanes are:

- Bharatanatyam/Kuchipudi: parents, teachers, arangetram/rangapravesam students, dance academies, and diaspora dance events.
- Deity/Varalakshmi: devotees, home mandir families, temple/community organizers, festival decorators, and South Indian diaspora families preparing for Varalakshmi, Navaratri, Diwali, and other alankaram seasons.

## Safe Golden Collections Positioning

Use these angles:

- Hyderabad/Secunderabad specialist in Bharatanatyam jewellery, Kuchipudi jewellery, deity jewellery, Hindu idol alankaram, real kemp temple jewellery, and kemp-style dance jewellery.
- Anil Tunk as the practical fit and selection guide for deity jewellery and dance jewellery.
- Practical guidance: photos, measurements, stage use, idol size, face visibility, crown width, haram drop, and set completeness.
- Regional naming authority: mukut/kireedam, vaddanam/oddiyanam, mattal/matil/mattel, ghungroo/salangai, namam/thiruman, haram, nethi chutti, rakodi, jada.
- First-hand proof where permission exists: Hanuman Jayanti alankaram at Sri Vijaya Vinayaka Swamy Temple, Malkajgiri; Varalakshmi alankaram examples shared with permission.

Avoid:

- Official supplier, temple-approved, priest-approved, endorsed, certified, preferred supplier, or guaranteed fit language unless separately confirmed in writing.
- Claiming Golden Collections was founded in 1961. Safe wording: Golden Collections started in 2012 and is rooted in a family jewellery tradition since 1961.
- Certificate claims.
- Ritual correctness claims for every temple, sampradaya, region, or family practice.

## First Pitch Ideas

### Narthaki / Sruti

Subject:

`Article idea: Bharatanatyam jewellery as aharya guidance for parents and dancers`

Core angle:

Golden Collections can contribute a practical, culturally respectful piece explaining how Bharatanatyam/Kuchipudi jewellery supports stage visibility, character, tradition, and practical readiness. The piece should explain set components and decision points, not sell products.

### India Currents / Khabar

Subject:

`Story idea: Why arangetram and festival jewellery still matter in diaspora homes`

Core angle:

For South Indian families outside India, jewellery for dance, puja, and festivals is part of cultural continuity. The story can combine practical planning, family memory, and the economics of preparing for arangetram, Varalakshmi, Navaratri, and Diwali.

### Hinduism Today

Subject:

`Article idea: Alankaram, beauty, and practical devotion in Hindu home worship`

Core angle:

An educational piece on how families approach deity adornment at home, why size and placement matter, and how devotees adapt temple-inspired alankaram respectfully in diaspora and urban homes.
