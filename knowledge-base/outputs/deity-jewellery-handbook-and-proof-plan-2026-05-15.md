How Golden Collections Helps You Choose Deity Jewellery Size

### Page Sections

#### 1. Direct Answer

Draft copy:

Golden Collections does not treat deity jewellery as one-size-fits-all. Before recommending a crown, mukut, kireedam, haram, necklace, vaddanam, earrings, namam, shanku chakra, hands, legs, arch, or altar accessory, we check the deity form, idol height, posture, dressing style, and placement area. Clear photos and measurements help us suggest the closest practical fit.

#### 2. Why Fit Changes From Idol To Idol

Draft points:

- Two idols of the same height may have different head width, shoulder width, waist placement, and chest space.
- A seated idol and standing idol need different ornament judgement.
- Dressing and flower decoration can change the final fit.
- Krishna, Ganesha, Lakshmi, Balaji, Durga, Murugan, Rama, Shiva, Sai Baba, and other deity forms may need different symbol and placement checks.
- A product can be beautiful but still unsuitable if the placement area is too small or too large.

#### 3. The Golden Collections Fit Guidance Process

Draft process:

1. Customer shares deity name, idol height, and required ornament type.
2. Customer sends front photo, side photo, close-up placement photo, and one photo with measuring tape.
3. Anil or the Golden Collections team checks the placement area and likely fit limits.
4. Suitable ready products are shortlisted where possible.
5. If ready products are not suitable, the customer is advised clearly instead of forcing a wrong fit.
6. Customer confirms order only after understanding fit notes, delivery expectation, and product limitations.

#### 4. Photos We Request Before Size Guidance

Draft copy:

For better guidance, send photos in natural light without heavy filters. Keep the idol straight in the frame. If the jewellery will be worn over dress or vastram, send one photo after dressing also.

Required photos:

- Full front photo of the idol.
- Side photo showing depth and posture.
- Close-up of head/face for crown, mukut, kireedam, namam, tilak, earrings, or nose ring.
- Close-up of neck/chest for necklace, short haram, long haram, mala, or pendant.
- Close-up of waist for vaddanam or oddiyanam.
- Photo with measuring tape visible.
- Photo of altar space if selecting arch, prabhavali, peedam, lotus base, banana tree, or background decor.

#### 5. Measurements We Ask For

Use this table on the page:

| Ornament type | Helpful measurements |
|---|---|
| Crown / mukut / kireedam | Idol height, head width, crown space height, head depth or circumference if available |
| Short necklace | Neck width, upper chest width, shoulder/dress volume |
| Long haram | Neck width, desired drop, chest height, dress thickness |
| Vaddanam / oddiyanam | Waist placement width after dressing |
| Earrings / nose ring | Ear or nose placement area and close-up photo |
| Namam / tilak / forehead piece | Forehead height and width |
| Shanku chakra / hands / legs | Placement area and deity posture |
| Arch / peedam / lotus / altar decor | Idol height, altar width, altar depth, background height |

#### 6. What We Can And Cannot Promise

Draft copy:

We can guide using product dimensions, experience, photos, and measurements. We cannot guarantee perfect fit from unclear photos, missing measurements, unusual idol posture, heavy dressing changes after purchase, or unconfirmed altar dimensions. When fit is uncertain, we will say so.

Draft content:

Before choosing any deity ornament, check:

1. Deity form and symbols.
2. Idol height and posture.
3. Exact placement area.
4. Dress, flowers, and altar space.

A correct choice usually comes from all four, not only from idol height.

#### Page 7: Crown, Mukut, And Kireedam Guide

Draft content:

For crown, mukut, or kireedam, measure idol height, head width, and available space above the head. Check whether the crown should sit in front, around the head, or behind the head. Existing hair, bun, peacock feather, flute, halo, or old crown may change the fit.

#### Page 8: Short Necklace Guide

Draft content:

A short deity necklace usually sits near the neck or upper chest. Check neck width, upper chest width, shoulder space, and dress thickness. If the idol has a broad chest or heavy vastram, the necklace may sit differently than expected.

#### Page 9: Long Haram Guide

Draft content:

Long haram selection depends on desired drop. Measure from neck to the point where the haram should end. Check whether the idol is standing or seated, and whether the haram should stay above the waist, touch the chest area, or sit over a garment.

#### Page 10: Vaddanam / Oddiyanam Guide

Draft content:

For vaddanam, measure the waist placement after dressing. A waist ornament that fits an undressed idol may become tight or unsuitable after saree, vastram, or flower decoration. Send a front photo and waist close-up.

#### Page 11: Earrings, Nose Ring, Tilak, Namam

Draft content:

Small face ornaments need close-up photos. For earrings, check ear placement and side space. For nose ring, check nose shape and placement. For tilak, namam, or thiruman, check forehead height, forehead width, and existing facial features.

#### Page 12: Shanku Chakra, Hands, Legs, And Deity Symbols

Draft content:

Symbol ornaments must match the deity form and placement. Check hand position, shoulder direction, posture, and available attachment area. Do not choose symbols only by size; deity form and visual balance also matter.

#### Page 13: Varalakshmi Alankaram Notes

Draft content:

For Varalakshmi, check face size, hands, legs, saree dressing, crown space, haram drop, and decor background. If using a doll, kalasham, or face set, confirm how the pieces attach and how the saree or blouse fabric affects final placement.

#### Page 14: Ganesha, Krishna, Balaji, Lakshmi, Durga And Other Forms

Draft content:

Different deity forms create different fit checks. Ganesha may need trunk and crown clearance. Krishna may need flute, feather, and shoulder space. Balaji may need namam and crown balance. Lakshmi and Durga may need crown, haram, and vaddanam coordination. Always send deity-specific photos.

#### Page 15: Altar Decor, Arch, Lotus, Banana Tree, And Peedam

Draft content:

For altar decor, measure the space, not only the idol. Check altar width, depth, background height, lamp position, flower space, and whether the item will stay permanently or only during festival alankaram.

#### Page 16: Common Mistakes To Avoid

Draft content:

Avoid choosing only by idol height. Avoid ordering without a measurement photo. Avoid assuming a standing idol ornament will suit a seated idol. Avoid measuring before dressing if the ornament will sit over fabric. Avoid buying face ornaments without close-up photos.

#### Page 17: What To Ask Before Ordering

Draft content:

Ask:

- Which ornament do I need first?
- Where should it sit on the idol?
- Is my photo clear enough for guidance?
- Is the idol dressed or undressed in the photo?
- Is there enough altar space?
- What fit uncertainty remains?
