## Common Measurement Block

Use this on every deity compatibility page, adjusted by deity:

Measure the idol and the exact placement area before choosing deity jewellery. Note idol height, head or face width, crown space height, neck or upper chest width, desired haram drop, waist placement width, ear/nose/forehead placement and altar or arch space. Send front and side photos with a measuring tape visible if fit is unclear.

Important caveat:

General deity jewellery is not automatically universal. Match the deity symbol, placement, size, posture and draping before ordering.

### Direct Answer

For Lakshmi, Varalakshmi and Amman idols, start with the setup type: full idol or doll, face/mugham, kalasam setup or goddess idol. Then choose crown, necklace/haram, earrings and vaddanam by measured size. ### Fit And Measurement Guidance

- Measure full idol or doll height, including crown, hair and decoration when the product is a finished Varalakshmi doll.
- Measure face width before choosing a face/mugham, crown or nose ornament.
- Measure crown space height and head/face width before choosing mukut, kireedam or hair crown.
- Measure neck or upper chest width for short necklaces.
- Measure desired haram drop for long harams and thomala/vagamalai placement.
- Measure waist placement after saree or vastram draping before choosing vaddanam.
- Measure altar depth and side space before choosing banana trees, lotus asana, coconut stands or arch items.

Special caveats:

- Hands and legs/hastham and padam are common in Varalakshmi, Lakshmi and Amman setups, but they are not universal.
- Lakshmi/Amman ornaments can overlap with Durga/Devi/Parvati by size and design, but weapons and symbol-specific accessories must remain explicit.

### Draft FAQs

**What jewellery is needed for Varalakshmi alankaram?**  
Most Varalakshmi setups start with a face or idol/doll, crown, short necklace or long haram, and hastham/padam when the body setup needs hands and feet. Earrings, nose ring, vaddanam, thomala, banana tree decor, lotus asana and coconut stand depend on the setup size and family tradition.

**Can Lakshmi jewellery be used for Amman or Devi idols?**  
Some Lakshmi, Amman and Devi jewellery can overlap when size, placement and design match. Do not treat deity-specific weapons, faces, hastham/padam or symbols as universal.

**How do I choose a crown for Lakshmi or Amman?**  
Measure idol height, face/head width, crown space height and crown depth. Also check hairstyle, saree drape and whether the crown is upright, tilted, full-round, open-back or hair-crown style.

**Are Varalakshmi hands and legs universal?**  
No. Hastham and padam pieces should be chosen by setup type, material, component count, height/length, width and placement. Some products include two hands only; some include two hands and two legs.

**Which decor items fit Varalakshmi Pooja?**  
Banana trees, banana bunches, lotus asana and coconut stands can support Varalakshmi Pooja setups when altar space and base size fit. Compare product height, length, width and placement before ordering.

## Schema Implementation Notes

Recommended JSON-LD graph per page:

1. `WebPage`
   - `name`: page title.
   - `description`: SEO/meta description or concise page summary.
   - `url`: canonical page URL.
   - `isPartOf`: Golden Collections website.
   - `about`: deity/deity group plus deity jewellery compatibility.

2. `BreadcrumbList`
   - Home.
   - Deity Jewellery and Alankaram Guides.
   - Current page.

3. `FAQPage`
   - Only include visible FAQ questions and answers.
   - Keep answers concise and factual.

4. Optional `ItemList`
   - Use for linked collections if rendered as a list of shopping paths.
   - Do not mark normal editorial links as Products unless product offer data is present.

5. Optional `HowTo`
   - Use only for a true step-by-step measurement section.
   - Do not force HowTo on pages that are mostly compatibility advice.

6. Optional `Person`
   - Use only when the page visibly says it was reviewed by Anil Tunk or links to `/pages/anil-tunk`.
   - Do not invent credentials.
